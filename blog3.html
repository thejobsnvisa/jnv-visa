Subclass 494 Visa

Supporting Regional Healthcare Workforce Needs

Last Updated: 10th September 2026

How Subclass 494 Helps Regional Healthcare Facilities Fill Critical Roles

Regional healthcare facilities across Australia continue to face significant workforce shortages. Hospitals, aged care homes, and community health centres in regional and remote areas often struggle to attract and retain qualified healthcare professionals.

The Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) Visa (Subclass 494) plays a crucial role in addressing this challenge by enabling regional employers to sponsor skilled overseas healthcare workers for critical roles.

Understanding the Subclass 494 Visa

The Subclass 494 visa allows approved regional employers to sponsor skilled workers to live and work in designated regional areas of Australia for up to five years. This visa is designed specifically to support regional industries that experience ongoing skill shortages, including healthcare.

For healthcare facilities, the Subclass 494 visa provides a reliable pathway to access qualified professionals such as nurses, doctors, allied health workers, and aged care specialists.

How Subclass 494 Supports Regional Healthcare Employers

Regional healthcare employers benefit from the Subclass 494 visa by gaining access to a wider international talent pool. Employers can fill roles that remain vacant locally, ensuring continuity of care and improved patient outcomes.

The visa also promotes workforce stability, as sponsored employees are required to work with their nominating employer in a regional area, reducing staff turnover.

Benefits for International Healthcare Professionals

For overseas healthcare workers, the Subclass 494 visa offers an opportunity to build a long-term career in Australia’s regional healthcare sector. Visa holders can live and work in regional communities while gaining valuable Australian work experience.

Importantly, the Subclass 494 visa also provides a clear pathway to permanent residency after meeting eligibility requirements, making it an attractive option for skilled professionals seeking stability and career progression.

Strengthening Regional Healthcare Through Skilled Migration

The Subclass 494 visa plays a vital role in strengthening Australia’s regional healthcare system. By enabling facilities to attract and retain skilled professionals, it helps improve service delivery and healthcare access for regional communities.
