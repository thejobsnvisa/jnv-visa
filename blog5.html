Labour Agreements

Flexible Migration Solutions for Healthcare Facilities

Last Updated: 10th September 2026

Benefits of Labour Agreements for Healthcare Facilities

Australia’s healthcare sector continues to face workforce shortages, particularly in aged care, regional hospitals, and specialised medical services. When standard skilled migration pathways are not sufficient, Labour Agreements offer healthcare facilities a flexible and strategic solution to recruit overseas professionals.

Labour Agreements allow approved healthcare employers to access skilled migrant workers under tailored conditions that better reflect their operational needs and local labour market realities.

What Is a Labour Agreement?

A Labour Agreement is a formal arrangement between an Australian employer and the government that allows the business to sponsor overseas workers under customised visa conditions. These agreements are particularly valuable for healthcare facilities experiencing ongoing and critical skill shortages.

Labour Agreements can cover occupations not available under standard visa programs or provide concessions on salary, skills, or English language requirements where justified.

Key Benefits for Healthcare Facilities

One of the biggest advantages of Labour Agreements is the ability to access a broader international talent pool. Healthcare employers can fill hard-to-staff roles that remain vacant for extended periods using local recruitment alone.

  • Access to overseas healthcare professionals for critical roles
  • Flexibility in visa requirements where approved
  • Improved workforce stability and continuity of care
  • Reduced reliance on short-term or agency staff

By securing long-term staffing solutions, healthcare facilities can focus on delivering consistent, high-quality patient care.

Supporting Aged Care and Regional Healthcare

Labour Agreements are particularly beneficial for aged care providers and regional healthcare facilities, where workforce shortages are most severe. These agreements enable employers to sponsor roles such as aged care workers, nurses, and support staff that are essential to daily operations.

For regional facilities, Labour Agreements help attract and retain skilled workers willing to commit to long-term employment, improving service delivery in underserved communities.
