Nurse Visa Comparison

Choosing the Right Visa for Healthcare Recruitment

Last Updated: 10th September 2026

Subclass 186 vs 494 vs 482: Which Visa Is Best for Hiring Nurses in Australia?

Australia’s healthcare sector continues to experience a strong demand for qualified nurses. Hospitals, aged care facilities, and regional health services often rely on skilled migration pathways to fill critical nursing roles. Among the most commonly used visas are Subclass 186, Subclass 494, and Subclass 482.

Each visa serves a different purpose. Choosing the right option depends on your location, workforce needs, and long-term staffing goals. This guide compares all three to help healthcare employers make informed decisions.

Subclass 186 – Employer Nomination Scheme (Permanent)

The Subclass 186 visa is a permanent employer-sponsored visa that allows Australian healthcare employers to nominate skilled nurses for permanent residency.

  • Best for: Long-term and permanent nurse recruitment
  • Visa duration: Permanent
  • Key benefit: Workforce stability and retention
  • Ideal roles: Registered Nurses, Clinical Nurses, Specialist Nurses

This visa is ideal for employers looking to build a stable nursing workforce. Nurses gain permanent residency, making them more likely to remain with the employer long term.

Subclass 494 – Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional)

The Subclass 494 visa is designed specifically for regional Australia. It allows approved regional healthcare employers to sponsor overseas nurses for up to five years.

  • Best for: Regional hospitals and aged care facilities
  • Visa duration: Up to 5 years
  • Key benefit: Pathway to permanent residency
  • Ideal roles: Aged Care Nurses, Community Health Nurses

This visa helps regional healthcare providers address chronic staff shortages while offering nurses the opportunity to settle and build careers in regional communities.

Subclass 482 – Temporary Skill Shortage (TSS)

The Subclass 482 visa is a temporary employer-sponsored visa used to address immediate nursing shortages. It allows employers to sponsor nurses for short- to medium-term roles.

  • Best for: Urgent or short-term nurse shortages
  • Visa duration: Up to 4 years (depending on stream)
  • Key benefit: Faster access to skilled nurses
  • Ideal roles: Registered Nurses, Specialist Nursing Positions

While the Subclass 482 visa is effective for quick recruitment, employers should plan ahead if permanent retention is the long-term objective.

Which Visa Should You Choose?

Choosing the right visa depends on your organisation’s needs:

  • Subclass 186: Best for permanent, long-term nurse employment
  • Subclass 494: Ideal for regional healthcare providers
  • Subclass 482: Suitable for temporary or urgent staffing needs
